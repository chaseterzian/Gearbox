Input fields are able to accept user defined parameters as follows:

Start time in milliseconds

End time in milliseconds

The ammount of movement present in the visuals that correspond to the X axis of the accelerometer (left and right latteral)

The ammount of movement present in the visuals that correspond to the Y axis of the accelerometer (braking and acceleration)

The ammount of movement present in the visuals that correspond to the Z axis of the accelerometer (up and down, bumps and dips)

Skip this amount of data points between each point while allowing data to still be presented in proper time (larger files, slower computers)

The number (N) entered represents N meters/second^2. When the force in the X direction (lateral force on the car) exceeds this number, the data will be dynamically printed in the "Show My Report" section

Report Trip Y: The number (N) entered represents N meters/second^2. When the force in the Y direction (braking and acceleration) exceeds this number, the data will be dynamically printed in the "Show My Report" section

Report Trip Z: The number (N) entered represents N meters/second^2. When the force in the Z direction (bumps and dips) exceeds this number, the data will be dynamically printed in the "Show My Report" section

The sensors on your smartphone are extremely sensitive. As a result, the different respresentations of the data can appear to be very jerky, and in some cases, difficult to interpret. The higher the number entered into this field, the more stable the graphic respresentations (pictures) of the data become. Although a higher number will produce 'cleaner' results, the data will be slightly less accurate, and will become more of an average as the number increases. Recommend less than 50

The higher the number entered into this field, the more stable the word print-outs become('driving straight' etc). Recommend less than 50

The higher the number entered into this field, the more stable the data print-outs become(X, Y, Z accelerometer data etc). Recommend less than 50
